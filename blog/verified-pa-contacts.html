How to Source Verified PA Contacts Without Wasting Budget

Every recruiter has felt this: you spend an afternoon building an outreach list, send your first sequence, and half of it bounces. The list looked fine. It just wasn't verified — and now your sender reputation has taken a hit before your first real conversation even started.

For Physician Assistant recruiting specifically, this problem is worse than average. PAs move between practices, health systems consolidate, and general-purpose contact databases update on their own schedule, not yours. A record that was accurate six months ago can be dead today.

What "verified" should actually mean

Not every database that claims to be verified means the same thing by it. At minimum, a contact should have been checked for:

  • Deliverability — has the mail server actually accepted mail for this address recently, not just "does it look like a valid email format."
  • Recency — when was this contact last confirmed, and is the record refreshed on an ongoing basis?
  • Professional vs. student status — are you reaching a practicing PA or a PA student, and is that clearly labeled before you spend a credit?

Why PA-specific data beats general databases

General recruiting databases mix PA contacts in with millions of records across every profession. You end up filtering more than recruiting. A database built specifically around the PA workforce — sourced through professional channels and verified on an ongoing basis — gets you to a usable list faster, with fewer dead ends.

That's the model Our PA contact database is built on: every contact is run through third-party deliverability verification before it's ever revealed to a subscriber, and professional and student records are clearly labeled so you know exactly who you're reaching.

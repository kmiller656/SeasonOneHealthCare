PA Job Growth Through 2034: What It Means for Recruiters

The numbers on the Physician Assistant workforce are hard to ignore. As of December 2025, there are roughly 201,000 certified PAs in the United States, according to the NCCPA. The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects 20% job growth for PAs between 2024 and 2034 — one of the fastest growth rates of any healthcare occupation — with roughly 12,000 new PA openings projected per year through the decade. Median total PA compensation sits around $140,000, per the AAPA's 2026 Salary Report.

What this means for sourcing, not just hiring

Fast-growing occupations create a specific recruiting problem: demand outpaces the number of active job seekers. When a role is genuinely competitive, the PAs worth reaching are often not actively browsing job boards — they're employed, doing fine, and only move for the right opportunity presented directly to them.

That shifts the job from "post and wait" to "identify and reach out." It means recruiters need two things a job board doesn't give you: a verified way to reach passive candidates directly, and a way to prioritize which opportunities are worth pursuing first.

Positioning for a growing market

A market growing at 20% doesn't reward recruiters who wait for candidates to come to them. It rewards recruiters with a direct line to verified contacts and a clear read on which facilities actually have the budget and mandate to hire right now — which is exactly what a PA-specific contact database paired with grant-funding data is built to provide.

About Us

Why We Started
Season One Healthcare

We built Season One Healthcare because we believe healthcare facilities — especially those serving underserved communities — deserve a staffing partner that takes their mission seriously.

Our Story

Staffing With Purpose

Season One Healthcare was founded on the belief that the staffing process should be straightforward, honest, and built around what a facility actually needs — not just what is easiest to deliver.

We specialize in permanent direct placement, working with hospitals, health centers, and clinics to find professionals who are not only qualified but genuinely aligned with the communities they will serve. We take the time to understand each facility's culture, patient population, and operational needs before presenting a single candidate.

The name reflects where we are: this is our first season. We are building something deliberately, one placement and one relationship at a time.

"The right professional in the right role changes patient outcomes. That is the only standard we hold ourselves to."

Who We Serve

The Facilities
We Partner With

We work with a range of healthcare organizations. Our particular focus is facilities in underserved and rural settings that are often underserved by larger national staffing firms.

01

Critical Access Hospitals

Critical Access Hospitals serve rural communities as the primary or sole source of acute care. Staffing these facilities requires professionals who are adaptable, scope-flexible, and genuinely committed to rural healthcare delivery. We understand those requirements and recruit accordingly.

02

Federally Qualified Health Centers

FQHCs provide care to underserved populations regardless of ability to pay. These organizations require professionals who are mission-aligned and understand the unique demands of community-based, patient-centered care. We work to find candidates who are the right fit, not just the right credential.

03

Rural Health Clinics

Rural Health Clinics operate in designated healthcare shortage areas and carry specific staffing requirements for advanced practice providers. We are familiar with these regulatory requirements and work to place candidates who meet them.

04

Tribal Health & Community Clinics

Tribally-operated health programs and independent community clinics serve populations with distinct needs and expectations. We approach these partnerships with respect for the communities involved and take additional care to identify candidates who are culturally aware and community-committed.

05

Independent & Specialty Clinics

We also work with independent practices and specialty clinics that need permanent staffing support without the overhead of a large national agency relationship. If your facility has an open role and needs a thoughtful staffing partner, we'd like to talk.

Nurse Practitioner Placement

NP Jobs in Rural
Washington State

Permanent Nurse Practitioner positions across rural Washington — a full practice authority state where NPs can practice to the full extent of their training.

Why Washington State

Full Practice Authority
for NPs in Washington

Washington is a full practice authority state for Nurse Practitioners, meaning NPs can evaluate, diagnose, and treat patients and prescribe medications independently, without a required physician collaboration agreement. For NPs seeking autonomy and a broad scope of practice, Washington is one of the strongest states in the country.

Rural facilities in Washington — critical access hospitals, rural health clinics, and federally qualified health centers — actively seek NPs who are willing to work in underserved communities. Rural Health Clinics specifically are federally required to maintain a PA or NP on-site at least 50% of operating hours, creating steady, consistent demand.

Season One Healthcare works with these facilities to find NPs who are not only clinically qualified but genuinely interested in the communities they will serve. We focus on permanent placement — not temporary coverage.

How we work

Direct Hire Is Different
From Travel Staffing

Most healthcare staffing arrangements are built around temporary coverage — travel nurses, locum tenens, per diem staff. The facility pays an ongoing agency rate, the clinician moves on after the assignment, and the cycle repeats. It solves a short-term problem but does not build a stable workforce.

Direct hire is different. Season One Healthcare sources, screens, and presents qualified candidates for permanent positions at your facility. You interview them, you make the hiring decision, and you bring them on as your own employee. We charge a one-time placement fee and our involvement ends there. No ongoing contracts, no recurring agency costs, no clinician who leaves after 13 weeks.

For facilities in the Pacific Northwest that are trying to build a stable, community-committed workforce, direct hire is the model that makes sense.
